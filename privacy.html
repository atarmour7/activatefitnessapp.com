Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 14, 2026  ·  Effective: July 14, 2026

Activate Fitness (the "App") is operated by ATA IT Services LLC, an Indiana limited liability company ("we," "us," or "our"). We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting it. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, how we share it, and the choices and rights you have.

Activate Fitness serves two kinds of users, and different rules apply to each:

  • Adults who use the App for themselves — you set your own activity goals and app restrictions to earn your own screen time.
  • Parents and guardians who manage children's use — you create and control profiles for your children, subject to the additional protections described in the "Children's Privacy (COPPA)" section below.

By downloading, accessing, or using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, do not use the App.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide

  • Account information: your email address and a numeric Admin PIN (stored only as a cryptographic hash — we never store the PIN itself).
  • Profile information: a first name for each profile, and for child profiles, an age or date of birth used to determine the appropriate consent flow.
  • Configuration you set: activity goals, selected apps or app categories to restrict, and schedules.

Information collected automatically with your permission

  • Activity data: daily step counts and Apple Watch Exercise-ring completion status, read from Apple Health (HealthKit) only after you grant permission. This data is used solely to calculate earned screen time and is not read for any other purpose.
  • Usage and enforcement data: screen-time balances, session start/stop records, and goal-completion records needed to operate the App.
  • Technical data: device identifiers, push-notification tokens (to deliver time-earned and time-low alerts), and diagnostic/crash data to keep the App reliable.

Information we do NOT collect

We do not collect precise geolocation, your contacts, photos, microphone or camera data, web-browsing history, or the content of your messages. We do not use third-party advertising identifiers or ad trackers.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information above only to: provide and operate the App's core feature (converting activity into earned screen time); apply the screen-time restrictions you configure; send you service-related notifications; maintain security, prevent fraud, and debug problems; comply with legal obligations; and, for child profiles, fulfill our obligations to parents under COPPA.

We do not use your information for advertising, and we do not use automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects about you.

3. How the App Enforces Screen Time (Apple Frameworks)

Activate Fitness uses Apple's Family Controls, Managed Settings, and Device Activity frameworks to restrict apps. For adult self-profiles, restrictions rely on Apple's individual Screen Time authorization, which the device owner can revoke in iOS Settings at any time. The App is a commitment tool and does not guarantee that restrictions cannot be removed. Data handled by Apple's frameworks is subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.

4. Adult Self-Profiles (Your Own Data)

If you create a profile for yourself, you are the subject of the activity and screen-time data that profile generates. We process it to provide the service to you. You can change your goals and restrictions at any time, and you can delete your account and all associated data as described in "Data Retention and Deletion."

5. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

Activate Fitness is designed so that child profiles are created and controlled exclusively by a parent or legal guardian. We comply with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act ("COPPA").

  • Verifiable parental consent: before any personal information is collected from a child under 13, we obtain verifiable parental consent and present a direct notice of our practices.
  • Data minimization: we collect only what is reasonably necessary for the service — a first name, an age, step counts, and screen-time records. We never condition a child's participation on providing more information than is necessary.
  • Parental rights: a parent may review the personal information collected from their child, request its deletion, and refuse to permit further collection, at any time, from the Admin dashboard or by contacting us. Deleting a child's profile deletes the associated personal information.
  • No disclosure to third parties: we do not disclose a child's personal information to third parties except the service providers described below who process it on our behalf to operate the App.

If you believe a child has provided us personal information without parental consent, contact us and we will delete it.

6. How We Share Information

We do not sell or rent personal information — of adults or children — and we have never done so. We share information only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: we use Google Firebase (database, authentication, cloud functions, and push notifications) and Resend (transactional email). These providers process data only to provide services to us under agreements that restrict their use of the data.
  • Legal requirements: we may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of our users or others.
  • Business transfers: if we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, information may be transferred, subject to this Privacy Policy and, for children's data, to continued COPPA compliance.

7. Data Retention and Deletion

Daily activity records are automatically deleted after 365 days. Account and profile data are retained while your account is active. You may delete your account and all associated profiles at any time in the App under My Account; we permanently delete the associated personal data from our active systems within 30 days, except where we are required by law to retain certain records (for example, records evidencing verifiable parental consent under COPPA). Backups are purged on our standard backup-rotation cycle.

8. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or receive a portable copy of your personal information, and to not receive discriminatory treatment for exercising these rights.

California residents (CCPA/CPRA)

In the preceding 12 months we have not sold or "shared" (as defined for cross-context behavioral advertising) personal information, and we do not do so — therefore no opt-out is necessary. California residents may exercise access, deletion, and correction rights by contacting us at the address below. We will verify your request using information associated with your account. We do not use or disclose sensitive personal information beyond the purposes permitted by law.

Other jurisdictions

Residents of other U.S. states with comprehensive privacy laws, and users in other regions, may have similar rights. Contact us and we will honor rights that apply to you.

9. Security

We protect information using industry-standard measures, including encryption in transit and at rest through our infrastructure providers, and storage of PINs only as cryptographic hashes. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. International Users

The App is operated from the United States and intended for U.S. users. If you access it from outside the United States, you consent to processing your information in the United States, where privacy laws may differ from those in your location.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the revised version here and update the "Last Updated" date. For material changes affecting children's personal information, we will notify parents and obtain new verifiable parental consent where required by law.
